About

retrosynthesis.com is a clean interface to the Odachi Retrosynthesis engine. It was based on principlies of simplicity, intuitiveness, and minimalism to allow chemists without formal computer science background to make use of the model's retrosynthetic prediction capabilities.

This site is powered by the Odachi Retrosynthesis Engine. Odachi uses a graph convolutional network to recognize retrosynthetically similar clusters within molecules. The network was trained over 400,000 examples from the USPTO reaction database. The network uses four stacked convolutional layers to generate a retrosynthetic embedding of the molecule and spectral clustering to identify the bond disconnection.

Frequently Asked Questions

  1. The disconnection returned makes no sense. Why?

    The Odachi Retrosynthesis Engine is still in its early stages, and reflects inherent biases in its training data. Often, the spectral clustering algorithm can incorrectly detect synthons. In this case, clicking RERUN will often help.

  2. How do I know this reaction will work in real life?

    The Odachi Engine running in the backend is designed to find topologically reasonable retrosynthetic disconnections. As of its current version, it does not validate the generated forward reactions for speed. Therefore, it is up to the user to use their judgement before attempting any reaction in the lab.
